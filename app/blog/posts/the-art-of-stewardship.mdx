## High Stakes

When I joined FundThrough, I didn't walk into a typical engineering job.

FundThrough is one of the leaders in the North American invoice factoring space, helping thousands of small businesses manage their cash flow. At this level, the engineering stakes are completely different. We aren't just playing with code; **every onboarding flow determines whether a small business owner gets paid this week or next.**

To commit to our mission, the product team needed a major refresh:

- Optimized client onboarding
- Faster funding
- AI-powered automation

But our frontend technology stack was not ready for what was coming.

---

## Legacy Empathy

We were running a five-year-old React application built on **Semantic UI** and **Webpack**.

It's easy for engineers to look at an older system and judge it. I believe it's far more important to look at it with **empathy**.

Every line of that legacy code was written to solve a real constraint at a specific moment in time. You could see that in the tens and hundreds of `if-else` conditions scattered throughout our components.

But yesterday's solutions had become today's bottlenecks:

- Build times were dragging
- Our UI library had no active development
- A tightly coupled global Redux state made shipping changes risky

The question wasn't just _“How do we write better code?”_  
It was: **“How do we modernize a mission-critical system without ever interrupting the mission?”**

---

## Surgical Strike

We couldn't afford a "Big Bang" rewrite that paused feature development for six months. The business was growing too fast for that.

Instead, I took a deliberate, **surgical approach to stabilization**:

- Moved from **Webpack to CRACO** to gain configuration flexibility
- Upgraded **Semantic UI** to its latest version to unblock rendering issues
- Upgraded **React** and **React Router**, enabling modern hooks and patterns

These upgrades weren't glamorous, but they bought us something priceless:  
**time and breathing room for the next 12–18 months.**

---

## Blank Slate

Once the foundation was steady, I stepped back and asked:

> **“If we started from scratch today, how would we build FundThrough's frontend?”**

We knew we needed a rebuild from the inside out:

- A true **design system**
- **TypeScript**
- **React Query**
- Modern tooling like **Vite**

Most importantly, we needed to do this **without slowing down the business for even a week.**

---

## The Bridge

We chose the **Strangler Fig** architectural pattern.

We moved to a **Turborepo monorepo** that allowed us to build “V2” features as packages. This was a game-changer.

It allowed us to:

- Build modern V2 components using our new stack
- Install and run those components **inside the legacy V1 app**

We achieved fast builds and high velocity, all while keeping the legacy flows completely intact. V1 kept the business running. V2 pulled the product into the future.

---

## The MVP

The common reflex in our industry is to look at a legacy system, want to burn it down, and start fresh on a greenfield.

But for a mature company, that implies a level of risk and investment the business **rarely needs**. FundThrough couldn't commit to a two-year rebuild while the market raced ahead. Leadership was rightfully skeptical of a “Big Bang” rewrite.

So instead of pitching a grand plan with a slide deck, I presented a **path**.

I built a **prototype**—a single login page.

- Built with **Vite, React, TypeScript, React Query, Tailwind CSS, and Radix UI**
- Embedded seamlessly into the legacy app via **Turborepo**
- Fast, clean, and modern
- Most importantly: **it didn't break production**

When I demonstrated this to the CTO, V2 stopped being a vague “hope” and became a tangible path. That prototype created immediate visibility and buy-in for **Product, Design, and Leadership.**

---

## What Matters

This is the part I'm most proud of, because it reflects **business success**, not just technical success.

From our recent report, the impact was clear:

- We saw **near 100% growth in users leveraging key in-app features** compared to the year before V2.
- **Onboarding completion rates increased** as customers moved through verification steps more confidently.
- Simultaneously, we saw **fewer “Can you do this for me?” calls** to Client Success.

By guiding users intuitively through the UI, we freed our Client Success team to focus on **growth instead of troubleshooting**.

We also unlocked innovation:

- We could now build and deploy **micro-apps (widgets)** directly into our partners' platforms, capturing client attention exactly where it matters most.
- The new platform made it far easier to support **AI-driven features** that would have been technically hard—or fragile—on the old stack.

Ultimately, this process **restored organizational trust** with Ops, Sales, and Leadership. We proved that Product & Engineering could tackle massive technical debt **while still delivering business value.**
